These ratios help us decide if the stock price is high or low compared to the company's earnings, sales, or growth potential.

  • A PEG significantly above 1 or 2 might suggest the stock price is high compared to expected growth.

P/S Ratio (Price-to-Sales): Compares the stock price (via market cap) to the company's total revenue. Useful when earnings are volatile. Lower is generally seen as cheaper relative to sales. Disney's P/S is 1.92.

EV/EBITDA: Compares the company's total value (Enterprise Value) to its operational earnings (EBITDA). Lower values are often preferred, suggesting the company might be cheaper relative to its core earnings power.

Is the Company Financially Healthy? (Health Ratios)

These ratios help us understand if the company manages its money well and isn't taking on too much risk.

Debt/Equity (D/E): Shows how much debt the company uses compared to its own funds (equity).

  • Lower D/E (e.g., below 100% or 1.0) generally means less financial risk.
  • Very high D/E means the company relies heavily on borrowing, which can be risky if business slows down.
Disney's D/E is 42.4%.

Current Ratio: Compares short-term assets (cash, receivables) to short-term debts.

  • Ratio > 1 means the company likely has enough liquid resources to cover immediate obligations (Generally good).
  • Ratio < 1 means short-term debts exceed easily accessible assets, which could indicate liquidity risk.
Disney's is 0.68.

Quick Ratio: Similar to Current Ratio, but excludes inventory (harder to sell quickly). A stricter test of immediate liquidity. Disney's is 0.55.

How Well is the Company Performing? (Profitability/Performance)

These metrics show how good the company is at making money from its business.

Profit Margin: How much profit the company keeps for every dollar of sales. Higher percentages are better, showing efficiency. Disney's margin is 6.1%.

ROE (Return on Equity): Shows how much profit the company generates using the money invested by shareholders. Higher percentages are generally better, indicating good returns for owners. Disney's ROE is 5.9%.

Dividend Yield: The annual dividend payment as a percentage of the stock price. This is direct cash return to shareholders. A higher yield means more income relative to price, but ensure the company can sustain the payments. Disney's forward yield is 1.02%.
